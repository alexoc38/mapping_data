ICE and CBP Data Shows Deportations Under Title 42 are Targetting the Same Immigrants as Before, in Greater Numbers

By: Alexandra O'Connor
Dec. 19, 2021

Under the authority of the Centers for Disease Control to combat the spread of COVID-19, U.S. Customs and Border Patrol began deportations under Title 42, but immigration advocates say it's working to deny migrants their legal right to be considered for asylum.

Daniel Tse works with the Haitian Bridge Alliance as their Black immigrant bail fund coordinator and works closely with the communities most vulnerable to immigration enforcement. Daniel is also a leader of the Cameroon Advocacy Network (CAN) where his coalition is working tirelessly to win Temporary Protective Status (TPS) for Cameroon.

“We have people who literally are living in fear every single day because of their experience through the asylum process, to the court rooms, their experiences from the ICE officers, CBP officers, whatever the case is because people who have been released either on parole or bond have even been deported. People usually cannot go out of the house because of the ankle monitors that we have on, or they come to the homes, or they're scared of ICE raids. This is the reality our communities are living.”
